Read or watch chapter 6 of Search Inside Yourself, and reflect:

Q: How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

A: It's pointed out that life through this lense has seasons of pleasure ie always chasing the next high (which is unsustainable), passion ie where you're in an enjoyable flow state, and higher purpose ie where you feel part of something bigger than yourself which is the most sustainable happiness.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise,

Q: What are your core values?

A: My core values wax around some key things, namely creativity ie generating ideas and bringing things to life, contributing ie making a difference to whatever I try my hand at, and coaching ie bringing along others with me.

Q: What do you stand for?

A: I try to have a sense of I guess honour about the way I get things done, in the hope that nice guys do finish first like Chade suggests. I also like to help others and make the people around me a bit happier for knowing me. It's the simple things right?

Q: How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

A: These intertwined ideas really resonate with me. There's lots of room for application in my former finance profession. You need to understand yourself and how to deal with your own foibles to get the outcome you want. This can take some strategising. When things go wrong you can also recognise the pattern to quickly get back on your feet again. Meditation is a way to interrogate situations and rehearse a positive response to them.

Q: Any other musings?

A: None at this time!
