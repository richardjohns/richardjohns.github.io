As if talking to a non-tech friend, tell a story about a time you:

1. Got blocked on a simple problem

Q: What was the problem?

A: In this case I was having trouble with a loop exercise and couldn't figure out why the loop wasn't adding to the variable.

Q: What problem solving techniques did you use?

A: Mainly I used psuedocode. I went for a walk and on the way back home with a coffee I realised why my variable wasn't increasing in value... it was due to me having placed the variable above the function (ie in the global scope). As a result the function loop kept grabbing the global variable, adding it then dropping it and starting from scratch each time. I put the variable inside the function and used a parameter then it started working... phew.

Q: How did you feel throughout the process?

A: Generally really frustrated but curious - alot of satisfaction when the code came right.

Q: What did you learn?

A: It was a simple example of scope chain that I learned from the hard way!

2. Solved a problem in an elegant way

Q: What was the problem?

A: I had problems with the chessboard idea and was unable to get an alternating pattern to console.log.

Q: What problem solving techniques did you use?

A: Again I mainly used psuedocoding to visualise a solution. I realised I could cheat by running the two types of lines (eg # first or space first) by running a nested loop formula (ie 4 inner loops and 4 outer loops).

Q: How did you feel throughout the process?

A: I knew it wasn't a perfect answer but frankly I was relieved that I had something I could use and progress with. I was pleased with at least having an answer.

Q: What did you learn?

A: I learned from the answer that the author's code was pretty complex - wasn't sure I knew enough about js to come up with that at my level. I did it my own way and produced a little thing I was proud of - though superficial!
